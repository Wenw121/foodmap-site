Black beans

Plant · legumes Lower methionine

Black beans provides 21.6 g of protein per 100 g, making it a high-protein food — about 21.6 g in a typical 100 g serving. Its protein-quality score (DIAAS) is 60, which is below the FAO threshold for a quality claim, and its first limiting amino acid is the sulfur amino acids (methionine and cysteine). Measured per gram of protein, it is low in methionine (15.0 mg/g) and provides 68.7 mg/g of lysine.
