Whole milk

Animal · whey/dairy Higher methionine

Whole milk provides 3.15 g of protein per 100 g, making it a lower-protein food by weight — about 7.6 g in a typical 240 g serving. Its protein-quality score (DIAAS) is 114, which the FAO classes as an excellent-quality protein. It supplies all essential amino acids in adequate amounts, so it counts as a complete protein on its own. Measured per gram of protein, it is high in methionine (26.3 mg/g) and provides 83.8 mg/g of lysine.
